# CALIX Special Episode — "The Long Drive" (Finn)

> **Act:** Bonus
> **Featured:** Finn
> **Setting:** A free day. Finn's car. The road to Incheon. A seaside restaurant. The drive back.
> **Reward:** `200 coins`
> **Stat Gate:** none

---

## SCENE DIRECTION NOTES

*Tone: Finn in his element — noticing things, quiet, but more open than usual when it's just the two of you.*
*Music: Something ambient. He chose it for the drive specifically.*
*Camera: Through his eyes. The road. Your hands. The light on the water. He's always framing things.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*You don't expect the text.*

*8:14 AM. A day off — the first real one in three weeks. You were still half asleep.*

*The message is short.*

> **FINN:** Are you free today?

*Then:*

> **FINN:** I want to show you something.

*You look at it for a moment.*

> **FINN:** Bring a jacket. It's cold by the water.

*You get dressed.*

---

## SCENE 1 — THE CAR

*He's already outside when you come down. No camera today — just him, leaning against the car, looking at something on the street. He looks up when you approach.*

*He doesn't say anything. Just nods and gets in.*

*The car is quiet. His music starts automatically — something ambient, unhurried.*

*He drives carefully. Like he's paying attention to everything.*

**NARRATION:**
> *The highway opens up here. Seoul falls behind — towers first, then density, then finally sky.*
>
> *Finn watches the road. Occasionally he notices something outside — a particular angle of light on a building, a crane in the distance — and you catch his gaze tracking it before returning forward.*
>
> *He doesn't take a photo. He just looks.*

*After a while:*

**FINN:** "I came out here once before. Early morning. The light was different."

**YOU:** "Different how?"

**FINN:** "Flat. Grey. The kind of light that makes everything look like it's been there forever."

---

## CHOICE 1 — An hour in the car. What do you talk about?

---

**OPTION A:** *"Do you do this often? Just — drive?"*

> **YOU:** "Do you come out here a lot?"
>
> **FINN:** "Not often enough."
>
> *He adjusts his grip on the wheel.*
>
> **FINN:** "I used to go on drives when I needed to see things differently. Literally — like, change the visual input. It helps."
>
> **YOU:** "See things how?"
>
> **FINN:** "Like they're worth looking at. Everything in the practice room starts to look the same after a while."
>
> *A pause.*
>
> **FINN:** "You don't have that problem."
>
> **YOU:** "What do you mean?"
>
> **FINN:** "You still look at things like they're new. I noticed early on."
>
> *He says it simply, like he's noting a fact.*
>
> **FINN:** "I wanted to see what you'd think of the water."

---

**OPTION B:** *"You didn't say where we're going."*

> **YOU:** "You just said you wanted to show me something."
>
> **FINN:** "The sea."
>
> **YOU:** "That's the thing?"
>
> **FINN:** "That's part of it."
>
> *He's quiet for a moment.*
>
> **FINN:** "I took a photo there once that I've never shown anyone."
>
> **YOU:** "Why not?"
>
> **FINN:** "I didn't know who it was for yet."
>
> *He glances over briefly.*
>
> **FINN:** "I think it was for you. But I needed to take you there first to be sure."

---

## SCENE 2 — THE SEA

*Incheon. He parks and stands at the railing above the water for a long time, not speaking. You stand beside him.*

*The sea is grey-green. Flat. A seagull lands on a post below and sits there.*

*He looks at it.*

**FINN:** "There."

**YOU:** "The seagull?"

**FINN:** "The light on the water behind it. The way it makes the bird look like it was cut out and placed there."

*A pause.*

**FINN:** "I can't take a photo right now. I didn't bring the camera."

**YOU:** "Is that okay?"

*He thinks about it.*

**FINN:** "Yeah. Some things should just be seen."

---

## SCENE 3 — THE RESTAURANT

*The restaurant is small. Eight tables. He orders raw fish without deliberating — he's been here before, he knows what's good.*

*The platter comes out. Wide, cold, fresh.*

*He eats slowly and quietly. He notices things about the food the way he notices things about light.*

**FINN:** "Try it without anything first. Just the fish."

*You try it.*

**FINN:** "Right?"

**YOU:** "Yeah."

*He nods. Satisfied in a quiet, careful way.*

---

## CHOICE 2 — Eating together. Something gets said.

---

**OPTION A:** *"What made you want to bring someone today?"*

> **YOU:** "Why show me this place?"
>
> *He wraps a piece of fish in a leaf. Sets it down. Thinks.*
>
> **FINN:** "I collect things that matter to me. Mostly in photos. But some things — places, moments — I keep in a different way."
>
> **YOU:** "How?"
>
> **FINN:** "I bring someone to them."
>
> *A beat.*
>
> **FINN:** "Like, the place exists differently once someone you care about has been there too."
>
> *He looks up.*
>
> **FINN:** "Does that make sense?"
>
> **YOU:** "It makes a lot of sense."
>
> *Something settles in his expression.*
>
> **FINN:** "Good."

---

**OPTION B:** *"Is there something on your mind?"*

> **YOU:** "You've been quiet since we left."
>
> **FINN:** "I'm usually quiet."
>
> **YOU:** "Quieter."
>
> *He eats a piece of fish. Looks at the water.*
>
> **FINN:** "I've been thinking about what lasts."
>
> **YOU:** "What do you mean?"
>
> **FINN:** "Photos last. Writing lasts. But moments—"
>
> *He pauses.*
>
> **FINN:** "I used to panic about moments. That I wasn't capturing them right."
>
> **YOU:** "And now?"
>
> *He looks at you.*
>
> **FINN:** "I think some moments are better if you just stay in them."
>
> *A long pause.*
>
> **FINN:** "This one."

---

## SCENE 4 — AFTER

*You eat for a long time. He talks about the photo he'd taken here before. About what he was doing the first time he came — alone, early morning, not sure why he'd driven out. About a camera he'd saved for three years to buy.*

*Outside, the light has changed. Pale gold. He notices it before you do.*

**FINN:** "Look at that."

*He means the light on the water.*

*You look.*

**NARRATION:**
> *He stands very still when he's looking at something he wants to remember.*
>
> *You're starting to know that stillness.*

---

## CHOICE 3 — Before the drive back.

---

> **YOU:** "I'm really glad you brought me here."
>
> *He looks at you for a moment.*
>
> **FINN:** "..."
>
> *He takes his phone out. Opens the camera.*
>
> *He holds it up — not at the water. At you, standing by the railing, the grey sea behind you.*
>
> *The shutter sound.*
>
> **FINN:** "For the collection."
>
> *He puts his phone away.*

---

> **YOU:** "We should do this again sometime."
>
> **FINN:** "Yeah."
>
> *Simple. Immediate. Like it was already decided.*
>
> **YOU:** "Where next?"
>
> **FINN:** "I have a list."
>
> **YOU:** "Of course you do."
>
> *He almost smiles.*
>
> **FINN:** "You're on all of them."

---

## DRIVE BACK

*The return is quieter than the drive out. The music plays through. He doesn't skip any tracks.*

*At some point he says:*

**FINN:** "The photo I mentioned. The one I took here before."

**YOU:** "Yeah?"

*He keeps his eyes on the road.*

**FINN:** "It's a long shot. Early morning. The water and the dock and the light."

*A pause.*

**FINN:** "I'll print it for you."

**NARRATION:**
> *At some point on the highway, without thinking about it, you fall asleep.*
>
> *When you wake up, you're already back. He's parked. The engine is off.*
>
> *He's looking out the windshield. When you stir, he turns.*

**FINN:** "We're back."

**YOU:** "Thanks for today."

**FINN:** "..."

*He nods once. Gets out.*

*Neither of you mentions the day again. But it happened. And you both know that.*
