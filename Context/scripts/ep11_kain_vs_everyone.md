# CALIX Episode 11 — "Kain vs. Everyone"

> **Act:** 2 — Friction
> **Featured:** Kain, Theo, Jay (Finn present, background)
> **Setting:** CALIX conference room. Label meeting fallout. Afternoon.
> **Reward:** 500 coins
> **Stat Gate:** GROUP_REP ≥ 3

---

## COLD OPEN

*The meeting was supposed to be an hour.*

*It was two and a half.*

*You know what the label decided before anyone says anything — you can read it in how they come out of the elevator. Kain first, face neutral, which means he's already absorbed a hit and closed around it. Theo close behind, jaw tight, which means he's furious and deciding how much of it to show. Jay last, hands in his pockets, which means he already saw this coming.*

*Finn looks at you when he sees you waiting.*

*He tilts his head once. Small. Not good.*

---

## SCENE 1 — THE ROOM

*They file into the conference room. Kain takes the head of the table out of pure habit. Theo doesn't sit — he stands at the window with his arms crossed.*

**THEO:**
"Okay. Can we talk about this now. Like actually talk."

**KAIN:**
"There's nothing to discuss. The decision's made."

**THEO:**
"The decision was made for us. That's a different thing."

**KAIN:**
"We knew this was possible when we signed the contract."

**THEO:**
"Knowing it was possible and it actually happening are two completely different —"

**KAIN:**
"Theo."

*A beat.*

**THEO:**
"No — Kain, you agreed without pushing back once. You sat in that room with them and you didn't push back on a single thing."

**KAIN:**
"There was no —"

**THEO:**
"There's always something. There's always a position. You chose not to take one."

*The room goes very still.*

*Jay is looking at the table. Not his phone. The table.*

*Finn is in the doorway, not quite in the room, watching Kain.*

---

## SCENE 2 — JAY

**JAY:**
"What was the pushback going to do."

*Everyone looks at him.*

**JAY:**
"Theo. Specifically — what was it going to accomplish."

**THEO:**
"We would have had a say. In our own —"

**JAY:**
"We had a say. We signed the contract. The contract said this was possible. Kain knew that going in. He was protecting something else."

*A pause.*

**THEO:**
"What something else."

*Jay looks at Kain.*

**JAY:**
"Tell him."

*Kain is very still.*

**KAIN:**
"There was a secondary clause. If we pushed back on this decision, it would have triggered a renegotiation of the renewal terms. The renewal that's coming up in eight months."

*Silence.*

**THEO:**
"..."

**KAIN:**
"I made a calculation. I took the loss on this to protect the renewal."

*Theo's arms uncross. He stares at Kain.*

**THEO:**
"You could have told us that."

**KAIN:**
"I'm telling you now."

**THEO:**
"Before. In the room, before you made the call."

**KAIN:**
"There wasn't time to —"

**THEO:**
"There was time. You just decided not to."

---

## SCENE 3 — THE FRACTURE

*The argument escalates.*

*Theo is not wrong. Kain is not wrong. That's the problem with this one.*

*Theo: decisions should be made together. A group is five people. Leadership doesn't mean calls made alone, and keeping things from the group is a different category of mistake than just making a wrong call.*

*Kain: someone has to see the whole board. In a room with label executives there is no time for consensus. His job is to protect the long game even when it costs the short one.*

*Two right answers hitting each other.*

*Jay says nothing after his initial statement. He watches.*

*Finn says nothing the whole time. He watches differently — the way you watch someone when you already know how the thing ends.*

*At the peak of it — everyone talking, Theo's voice up, Kain's voice going quieter which means he's more certain not less — Kain says:*

**KAIN:**
"This isn't productive."

*And Theo says:*

**THEO:**
"Because you won't hear anything you didn't already decide before you walked in."

*And then it's quiet.*

*And Kain looks at you.*

---

## SCENE 4 — AFTER

*The room empties slowly.*

*Theo goes first — not storming, just needing to move. Jay follows him. You know Jay thinks Theo needs company.*

*Finn disappears the way he came in — quietly, completely.*

*You're the last one out. Kain is still at the head of the table.*

**KAIN:**
"I knew about the renewal clause for six weeks."

*He says it to the room. You're still in it.*

**KAIN:**
"I should have told them when I found out."

*A pause. He turns his pen over in his hands — the handwritten one, not his tablet stylus.*

**KAIN:**
"I don't know when to share things and when to carry them. I always think I'm protecting the group. I think I'm usually protecting myself."

*He sets the pen down.*

**KAIN:**
"That's not a leadership quality. I'm aware."

*He stands up.*

*He's taller standing than sitting — you notice this every time — and for a second you're both just in the conference room with the afternoon light coming through the window, and there's no performance in him at all.*

**KAIN:**
"I'm working on it."

*He walks out.*

**NARRATION:**
> *Kain in an empty conference room, turning his pen over, saying: I'm working on it.*
>
> *You stand there for a moment before you follow.*
