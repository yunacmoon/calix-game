# CALIX Episode 11 — "Kain vs. Everyone"

> **Act:** 2 — Friction
> **Featured:** Kain, Theo, Jay (Finn present, background)
> **Setting:** CALIX conference room. Label meeting fallout. Afternoon.
> **Reward:** 500 coins
> **Stat Gate:** GROUP_REP ≥ 3

---

## COLD OPEN

*The meeting was supposed to be an hour.*

*It was two and a half.*

*You know what the label decided before anyone tells you — you can read it in how they come out of the elevator. Kain first, face neutral, which means something went wrong and he's already absorbed it. Theo close behind, jaw tight, which means he's furious and trying to decide how furious. Jay last, hands in pockets, which means he already knew this was going to happen.*

*Finn looks at you when he sees you waiting.*

*He tilts his head once. Small — like: not good.*

---

## SCENE 1 — THE ROOM

*They file into the conference room. Kain sits at the head of the table out of pure habit. Theo doesn't sit. He stands at the window with his arms crossed.*

**THEO:**
"Okay. Can we talk about this now."

**KAIN:**
"There's nothing to discuss. The decision is made."

**THEO:**
"The decision was made for us. That's different."

**KAIN:**
"We knew this was possible when we signed the contract."

**THEO:**
"Knowing it was possible and it actually happening are two —"

**KAIN:**
"Theo."

*A beat.*

**THEO:**
"No — Kain, you agreed without even pushing back. You sat in that room and you didn't push back once."

**KAIN:**
"There was no —"

**THEO:**
"There's always something. There's always something to push back on. You just chose not to."

*The room is very still.*

*Jay is looking at the table. Not on his phone. Looking at the table.*

*Finn is in the doorway, not quite in the room, watching Kain.*

---

## SCENE 2 — JAY

**JAY:**
"What was the pushback going to do."

*Everyone looks at him.*

**JAY:**
"Theo. What specifically was the pushback going to accomplish."

**THEO:**
"We would have had a say. In our own —"

**JAY:**
"We had a say. We signed the contract. The contract said this was possible. Kain knew that. He was protecting something else."

*A pause.*

**THEO:**
"What something else."

*Jay looks at Kain.*

**JAY:**
"Tell him."

*Kain is very still.*

**KAIN:**
"There was a secondary clause. If we pushed back on this decision, it triggered a renegotiation of the renewal terms. The renewal that's coming up in eight months."

*Silence.*

**THEO:**
"..."

**KAIN:**
"I made a calculation. I took the loss on this to protect the renewal."

*Theo's arms uncross. He stares at Kain.*

**THEO:**
"You could have told us that."

**KAIN:**
"I'm telling you now."

**THEO:**
"Before. In the room."

**KAIN:**
"There wasn't time to —"

**THEO:**
"There was time. You just decided not to."

---

## SCENE 3 — THE FRACTURE

*The argument escalates.*

*Theo is not wrong. Kain is not wrong. That's the problem.*

*Theo is right that decisions should be made together — that a group is five people, that leadership doesn't mean making calls alone, that keeping things from the group is a different category of mistake than making a wrong call.*

*Kain is right that someone has to see the whole board, that in the moment of a meeting with label executives there is no time for group consensus, that his job is to protect the long game even when it costs the short one.*

*Two right answers. One argument.*

*Jay says nothing after his initial statement.*

*Finn says nothing the whole time.*

*At the peak of it — everyone talking, Theo's voice rising, Kain's voice getting quieter which means he's more certain not less — Kain says:*

**KAIN:**
"This isn't productive."

*And Theo says:*

**THEO:**
"Because you won't hear anything you didn't already decide."

*And then it's quiet.*

*And Kain looks at you.*

---

## CHOICE — In the silence after Kain ends it, he looks at you. The only one who hasn't spoken.

---

**OPTION A:** Side with Theo out loud.
> **YOU:** "He's right that you should have said something in the room. Even fast — even just so we knew."
>
> *Kain holds eye contact with you.*
>
> **KAIN:** "..."
>
> *He doesn't answer. He caps his pen.*
>
> *Theo exhales — not triumphant, just released.*
>
> **THEO:** "Thank you."
>
> **YOU (internal):** *Kain will remember this. Not with resentment. With information.*
>
> `[+2 THEO_TRUST] [-2 KAIN_TRUST]`

---

**OPTION B:** Side with Kain out loud.
> **YOU:** "He made the call he thought protected the group. That's what a leader does when there's no time."
>
> *Theo stares at you.*
>
> **THEO:** "..."
>
> *Jay looks up from the table.*
>
> **KAIN:** *(very quietly)* "..."
>
> *He nods once. It's not gratitude — it's acknowledgment.*
>
> `[+2 KAIN_TRUST] [-1 THEO_TRUST] [-1 JAY_TRUST]`

---

**OPTION C:** Say nothing. Make eye contact with Kain.
> *You hold his gaze.*
>
> *You don't agree. You don't disagree. You just look at him — fully — and let him be seen.*
>
> *A moment.*
>
> *Kain looks away first. At the table.*
>
> **KAIN:** "I'll tell the group before the next meeting. Whatever the information is."
>
> *It's not an apology. It's a policy change. In Kain's language, that's the same thing.*
>
> *Theo, quietly:*
>
> **THEO:** "Okay."
>
> `[+2 KAIN_TRUST] [+1 JAY_TRUST]`

---

**OPTION D:** Suggest a compromise.
> **YOU:** "Can we — what's the thing we actually want going forward? Not what happened today. What's the process for next time."
>
> *A pause.*
>
> *Everyone refocuses.*
>
> **JAY:** "In-room decision rights. If something major comes up, Kain can make the call but has to flag it to the group within an hour."
>
> *Theo looks at Jay.*
>
> **THEO:** "...yeah. That works."
>
> *Kain looks at the table. Then:*
>
> **KAIN:** "Agreed."
>
> `[+1 GROUP_REP] [+1 KAIN_TRUST]`

---

## SCENE 4 — AFTER

*The room empties slowly.*

*Theo goes first — not storming, just needing to move. Jay follows him, which is how you know Jay thinks Theo needs company.*

*Finn disappears the way he came in — quietly, completely.*

*You're the last one out. Kain is still at the table.*

**KAIN:**
"I knew about the renewal clause for six weeks."

*He says it to the room. You happen to be in it.*

**KAIN:**
"I should have told them."

*A pause.*

**KAIN:**
"I don't — I don't know when to share things and when to carry them."

*He says it like a problem he's trying to solve.*

**KAIN:**
"That's not a leadership skill I have."

*He picks up his pen. Stands.*

**KAIN:**
"I'm working on it."

*He walks out.*

**NARRATION:**
> *Kain admitting to a gap in himself, to you, in an empty conference room — that's not small.*
>
> *You'll think about it for a while.*
