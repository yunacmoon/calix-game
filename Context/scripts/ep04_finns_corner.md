# CALIX Episode 04 — "Finn's Corner"

> **Act:** 1 — Arrival
> **Featured:** Finn
> **Setting:** Dorm, common room. 2am. Everyone else is asleep. Rain outside — not dramatic rain, just steady, the kind that's been going for hours.
> **Reward:** Candy bar (from Finn)
> **Stat Gate:** None

---

## SCENE DIRECTION NOTES

*Tone: 2am blue. The specific quality of a conversation that only happens because everyone else is asleep.*
*Music: Nothing with rhythm. One ambient note, long and low, like a room breathing.*
*Camera: Close on small things. The rain on the window. The page of the book. Hands around cups.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*2:06am.*

*You've been lying in bed for an hour and a half.*

*It's not nerves exactly — you've gotten past the shaking kind of first-week nerves. It's more the specific restlessness of too much information, too many faces, too many things you're still figuring out how to read. The room isn't unfamiliar anymore. But you're not comfortable yet either. You're in the gap.*

*You get up.*

*The common room is dark except for one lamp in the far corner — the small one by the window, the one that nobody uses during the day because the ceiling lights overpower it.*

*Someone is in the corner chair.*

*Finn.*

*He has a book open on his knee and a half-empty glass of water on the side table. He's reading, or he was — when you come in, he looks up.*

*He doesn't look startled. He looks like he was waiting for something, not specifically you, but something.*

**FINN:**
"Hey."

*He says it the way he says everything — quietly, like he's made room for the word before he uses it.*

---

## SCENE 1 — THE CORNER

*You hover in the doorway.*

**FINN:**
"You don't have to stand there."

*He says it without pressure. Just as information.*

*You come in. You sit on the couch, which puts you at a right angle to his chair. Not close, not far. The lamp is between you.*

*He goes back to his book.*

*You sit.*

*The rain sounds louder inside than it did from your room.*

*A minute passes. Maybe two.*

**FINN:**
"What are you reading?"

*He doesn't look up.*

**YOU:**
"I'm not reading anything. I can't sleep."

*He turns a page.*

**FINN:**
"I know. I meant — what do you read, generally. When you do."

*He glances up briefly, settles back into the page.*

**FINN:**
"I find I can tell a lot about someone from what they come back to."

---

## SCENE 2 — WHAT DO YOU READ

*You think about it.*

**YOU:**
"I don't read as much as I should."

**FINN:**
"Nobody does. That's not what I asked."

*He turns another page. He reads fast — or he's been through this book before.*

**YOU:**
"Things that feel true. Specific stories about specific people — not big themes."

*Finn pauses. Not a full stop, just a half-beat.*

**FINN:**
"I like that."

*He dog-ears his page. Closes the book.*

**FINN:**
"There's a kind of writing that's trying to be Important with a capital I and you can feel it on every page. Like the author is watching themselves write."

*He sets the book on the table.*

**FINN:**
"And then there's the other kind. Where it feels like they forgot they were writing and they're just — saying something."

*He picks up his glass.*

**FINN:**
"Jay writes like the second kind. Did you know that?"

*He looks at you. Not a test — genuine curiosity.*

**FINN:**
"His lyrics, I mean. They don't sound like someone trying to be good. They sound like someone who ran out of patience for anything that isn't true."

*He takes a sip.*

**FINN:**
"I think that's the best thing you can say about anyone's work."

---

## SCENE 3 — THE QUESTION

*The rain shifts — slightly heavier, then steadies again.*

*Finn has been quiet for a few minutes, which is not unusual. His silences don't ask anything of you. You've noticed that. Most people's silences are a request: say something, fill this, make this comfortable. Finn's silences just exist.*

*Which is why it lands differently when he speaks.*

**FINN:**
"Can I ask you something?"

*He looks at you directly.*

**FINN:**
"You don't have to answer. But I want to ask it for real, not — not the way people ask things when they already know what they want you to say."

*He waits for you to nod.*

**FINN:**
"What made you want this?"

*He holds the question in the air.*

**FINN:**
"Not CALIX specifically. This — the industry, the work, all of it. The thing you're actually chasing."

## SCENE 4 — KAIN

*The conversation turns — gently, naturally — to the group.*

**FINN:**
"How are you reading Kain?"

**YOU:**
"Honestly?"

**FINN:**
"Please."

**YOU:**
"Like someone who built a system and doesn't know what to do when the system has to expand."

*Finn looks at you for a second.*

**FINN:**
"That's — actually very accurate."

*He turns the book over in his hands.*

**FINN:**
"He's not cold. He's just — Kain has a very specific idea of what CALIX is supposed to be, and that idea took years to build, and it cost him things. So when anything threatens it — even good things — his first instinct is protection."

*He pauses.*

**FINN:**
"He does that thing where he doesn't let himself want something until he knows he can have it. So he won't let himself want you in the group until he's sure you're staying."

**YOU:**
"And if I prove I'm staying?"

**FINN:**
"Then he'll want it so much it'll surprise him."

*He says it simply. Like he's read ahead.*

---

## SCENE 5 — THEO AND JAY

*You ask, because you're curious:*

**YOU:**
"What about Theo and Jay?"

*Finn almost smiles.*

**FINN:**
"Theo is exactly what he looks like. That's rare. He's genuinely warm, genuinely competitive, genuinely delighted when someone is good. He doesn't have a version of himself that's performing — that is the performance, all the way through."

*He tilts his head.*

**FINN:**
"Jay is the opposite. Jay has more layers than he lets on and he only shows you one at a time. You have to earn each layer separately. But once you've earned one —"

*He pauses.*

**FINN:**
"He was in the hallway writing tonight. He sat out there for two hours. The fact that he spoke to you at all means you already got one."

*He looks at you levelly.*

**FINN:**
"Don't waste it."

---

## SCENE 6 — GETTING LATE

*It's past 3am. You both notice around the same time.*

*Finn closes his book for real — no dog-ear this time, he uses a receipt he finds in his pocket as a bookmark.*

**FINN:**
"You should sleep."

**YOU:**
"So should you."

**FINN:**
"I will. I just think better at night."

*He stands. He's taller than he looks when he's sitting.*

*He goes to the kitchen — you hear a cupboard open, a rustle.*

*He comes back and puts something on the couch cushion next to you.*

*A chocolate bar. The kind with almonds. Half-eaten — no, the other half is still in its wrapper, folded neatly.*

**FINN:**
"The other half is mine. From earlier."

*He looks at it, then you.*

**FINN:**
"The whole one is yours."

*He picks up his book.*

**FINN:**
"Goodnight."

*He disappears down the hall.*

---

## SCENE 7 — AFTER

*You sit in the common room alone for a while.*

*The lamp is still on. The rain is still going.*

*You eat the chocolate bar — it's good, dark with salt — and listen to the building settle.*

**NARRATION:**
> *Finn, you decide, is the kind of person who understands things before they're explained to him. It should be unsettling. It isn't. Or maybe it is, and you don't mind.*
>
> *He told you things about Kain and Jay and Theo that felt true in the specific way that only things from outside your own head can feel true — because you were thinking them and didn't have the words yet.*
>
> *You think: if this group is going to work, and if you're going to be the fifth person in it, you will need Finn more than you initially understood.*
>
> *You go to bed.*
>
> *This time, you sleep.*
