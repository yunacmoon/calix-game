# CALIX Episode 04 — "Finn's Corner"

> **Act:** 1 — Arrival
> **Featured:** Finn
> **Setting:** Dorm, common room. 2am. Everyone else is asleep. Rain outside — not dramatic rain, just steady, the kind that's been going for hours.
> **Reward:** Candy bar (from Finn)
> **Stat Gate:** None

---

## SCENE DIRECTION NOTES

*Tone: 2am blue. The specific quality of a conversation that only happens because everyone else is asleep.*
*Music: Nothing with rhythm. One ambient note, long and low, like a room breathing.*
*Camera: Close on small things. The rain on the window. The page of the book. Hands around cups.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*2:06am.*

*You've been lying in bed for an hour and a half.*

*It's not nerves exactly — the shaking kind passed. It's more the restlessness of too much information, too many things you're still learning to read. The room isn't unfamiliar anymore but you're not comfortable yet either. You're in the gap.*

*You get up.*

*The common room is dark except for one lamp in the far corner — the small one by the window, the one that nobody uses during the day because the ceiling lights overpower it.*

*Someone is in the corner chair.*

*Finn.*

*He has a book open on his knee and a half-empty glass of water on the side table. He's reading, or he was — when you come in, he looks up.*

*He doesn't look startled. He looks like he was waiting for something, and here you are.*

**FINN:**
"Hey."

---

## SCENE 1 — THE CORNER

*You hover in the doorway.*

**FINN:**
"You don't have to stand there."

*You come in. You sit on the couch — at a right angle to his chair, not close, not far. The lamp is between you.*

*He goes back to his book.*

*The rain sounds louder in here than it did from your room.*

*A minute passes. Maybe two.*

**FINN:**
"What are you reading?"

*He doesn't look up.*

**YOU:**
"I'm not reading anything. I can't sleep."

*He turns a page.*

**FINN:**
"I know. I meant — what do you read, generally. When you do."

*He glances up briefly.*

**FINN:**
"I find I can tell a lot about someone from what they keep coming back to."

---

## SCENE 2 — WHAT DO YOU READ

*You think about it.*

**YOU:**
"I don't read as much as I should."

**FINN:**
"Nobody does. That's not what I asked."

*He turns another page. He reads fast — or he's been through this book before.*

**YOU:**
"Things that feel true. Specific stories about specific people. Not big themes."

*Finn pauses. Not a full stop — just a half-beat, like something landed.*

**FINN:**
"I like that."

*He dog-ears his page. Closes the book.*

**FINN:**
"There's a kind of writing that's trying so hard to be Important — capital I — that you can feel it pressing through every sentence. Like the writer is watching themselves write and making sure you know it."

*He sets the book on the table.*

**FINN:**
"And then there's the other kind. Where it feels like they forgot they were writing at all and they're just — saying something. To someone."

*He picks up his glass.*

**FINN:**
"Jay writes like the second kind. Did you know that? His lyrics, I mean. They don't sound like someone trying to be good. They sound like someone who ran out of patience for anything that isn't true."

*He looks at you.*

**FINN:**
"I think that's the best thing you can say about anyone's work."

---

## SCENE 3 — THE QUESTION

*The rain shifts — slightly heavier, then steadies.*

*Finn has been quiet for a few minutes, which is not unusual. His silences don't ask anything of you. Most people's silences are a small request: fill this, make it comfortable. His just exist.*

*Which is why it lands differently when he speaks.*

**FINN:**
"Can I ask you something?"

*He looks at you directly.*

**FINN:**
"You don't have to answer. But I want to ask it properly — not the way people ask things when they already know what they want you to say."

*He waits for you to nod.*

**FINN:**
"What made you want this?"

*A pause.*

**FINN:**
"Not CALIX specifically. This — the industry, the work, all of it. The thing underneath all of it that you're actually chasing."

## SCENE 4 — KAIN

*The conversation turns — gently, naturally — to the group.*

**FINN:**
"How are you reading Kain?"

**YOU:**
"Honestly?"

**FINN:**
"Please."

**YOU:**
"Like someone who built a system and doesn't know what to do when the system has to expand."

*Finn looks at you for a moment.*

**FINN:**
"That's — actually very accurate."

*He turns the book over in his hands.*

**FINN:**
"He's not cold. He has a very specific idea of what CALIX is supposed to be, and that idea took years and it cost him things. So when anything threatens it — even good things — his first instinct is to hold it together."

*He pauses.*

**FINN:**
"He doesn't let himself want something until he knows he can have it. So he won't let himself want you in the group until he's certain you're staying."

**YOU:**
"And if I prove I'm staying?"

**FINN:**
"Then he'll want it so much it'll surprise him."

*He says it simply. Like he's read ahead.*

---

## SCENE 5 — THEO AND JAY

**YOU:**
"What about Theo and Jay?"

*Finn almost smiles.*

**FINN:**
"Theo is exactly what he looks like. That's rare — genuinely rare. He's warm all the way through, not just on the surface. He doesn't have a version of himself that's performing warmth. That is the performance, top to bottom."

*He tilts his head.*

**FINN:**
"Jay is the opposite. More layers than he shows you, and he shows you one at a time. You have to earn each one separately. But once you have one —"

*He pauses.*

**FINN:**
"He sat in that hallway writing for two hours tonight. The fact that he spoke to you at all means you already got one."

*He looks at you.*

**FINN:**
"Don't waste it."

---

## SCENE 6 — GETTING LATE

*It's past 3am. You both notice at the same time.*

*Finn closes his book — no dog-ear this time, he uses a receipt from his pocket as a bookmark.*

**FINN:**
"You should sleep."

**YOU:**
"So should you."

**FINN:**
"I will. I just think better at night. Always have."

*He stands. He's taller than he looks sitting down.*

*He goes to the kitchen — a cupboard opens, a rustle.*

*He comes back and sets something on the couch cushion beside you.*

*A chocolate bar. The kind with almonds. Half-eaten — the other half still in its wrapper, folded neatly.*

**FINN:**
"The other half's mine. From earlier. The whole one is yours."

*He looks at it briefly, then you.*

**FINN:**
"Goodnight."

*He disappears down the hall.*

---

## SCENE 7 — AFTER

*You sit in the common room alone for a while.*

*The lamp is still on. The rain is still going.*

*You eat the chocolate bar — it's good, dark with salt — and listen to the building.*

**NARRATION:**
> *The thing about Finn is that he understands things without needing them explained. It should feel like too much. Somehow it doesn't.*
>
> *He told you things about Kain and Jay and Theo that felt true the way things only feel true when they come from outside your own head — because you were already thinking them, and didn't have the words yet.*
>
> *You go to bed.*
>
> *This time, you sleep.*
