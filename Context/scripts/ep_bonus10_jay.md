# CALIX Special Episode — "The Long Drive" (Jay)

> **Act:** Bonus
> **Featured:** Jay
> **Setting:** A free day. Jay's car. The road to Incheon. A seaside restaurant. The drive back.
> **Reward:** `200 coins`
> **Stat Gate:** none

---

## SCENE DIRECTION NOTES

*Tone: Jay unexpectedly loose. The version of him that exists when he doesn't have to perform distance.*
*Music: One album, start to finish, no skipping. He notes which tracks you react to.*
*Camera: The road ahead. His profile. The way he looks at the water like he's reading something.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*You don't expect the text.*

*8:14 AM. A day off — the first real one in three weeks. You were still half asleep.*

*One message. No context.*

> **JAY:** You awake?

*Then, after a pause:*

> **JAY:** I'm driving to the sea. If you want to come, I'm leaving in twenty minutes.

*You stare at it.*

*Twenty minutes doesn't leave room for thinking about it.*

*You get dressed.*

---

## SCENE 1 — THE CAR

*His car smells faintly of paper. There's a book in the back seat — spine up, open to a page. You don't look at what page.*

*He has one album already playing when you get in. He doesn't explain it.*

*He drives like someone who does this a lot — comfortable with the long stretches, no hurry.*

*The city thins out.*

**NARRATION:**
> *The highway opens up here. Seoul falls behind — towers first, then density, then finally sky.*
>
> *Jay doesn't talk for the first thirty minutes. It's not uncomfortable. It feels like he's giving you the drive first before anything else.*

*Then:*

**JAY:** "You ever been out this way?"

**YOU:** "Not really."

**JAY:** "The land flattens out. I like it."

---

## CHOICE 1 — An hour in the car. What do you talk about?

---

**OPTION A:** *"Do you do this often? Just — drive?"*

> **YOU:** "Do you come out here often?"
>
> **JAY:** "When I need to think through something."
>
> **YOU:** "What are you thinking through today?"
>
> *A pause. He watches the road.*
>
> **JAY:** "I'm not sure yet. Sometimes I drive to find out."
>
> **YOU:** "And you'll know by the time you get back?"
>
> **JAY:** "Usually. The distance helps."
>
> *He adjusts the volume slightly.*
>
> **JAY:** "There's a line I like — 'the mind thinks better when the body is in motion.'"
>
> **YOU:** "Who wrote it?"
>
> **JAY:** "I don't remember. Or maybe I did. I'm not always careful about the line between what I've read and what I've thought."
>
> *He glances over briefly.*
>
> **JAY:** "You can fall asleep if you want. I don't mind driving in quiet."
>
> **YOU:** "I'm not tired."
>
> **JAY:** "I know. I'm saying you can."
>
> *He says it like a door, left open.*

---

**OPTION B:** *"You didn't say where we're going."*

> **YOU:** "You didn't say where, exactly."
>
> **JAY:** "Incheon. There's a place by the water."
>
> **YOU:** "Have you been before?"
>
> **JAY:** "Once. On my own."
>
> **YOU:** "What made you want to go back?"
>
> *He thinks about it.*
>
> **JAY:** "Some places are better the second time. You're not figuring out the logistics anymore. You can just be there."
>
> *A pause.*
>
> **YOU:** "And you wanted company this time."
>
> **JAY:** "I thought about it for a few days."
>
> **YOU:** "About who to bring?"
>
> **JAY:** "About whether to bring anyone."
>
> *He looks at the road.*
>
> **JAY:** "I decided yes."
>
> *A beat.*
>
> **JAY:** "And then — you."

---

## SCENE 2 — THE SEA

*Incheon. The industrial port gives way to a quieter stretch. He parks and walks to the railing and stands there with his hands in his pockets, looking at the water for a long time.*

*You stand beside him.*

**NARRATION:**
> *The sea here isn't dramatic. Grey-green. Flat. Seagulls. The sound of boats.*
>
> *Jay looks at it like he's reading something.*

**JAY:** "I wrote something here the first time I came."

**YOU:** "What?"

**JAY:** "Just — a line. For a song that doesn't exist yet."

**YOU:** "What was the line?"

*He's quiet for a moment.*

**JAY:** "I'll tell you later. Let's eat."

---

## SCENE 3 — THE RESTAURANT

*The restaurant is small. Eight tables. He orders without looking at the menu — he's clearly thought about this. The raw fish comes out on a wide platter, fresh, cold.*

*He eats carefully. He pays attention to what he's eating in a way that feels deliberate, like everything else he does.*

**JAY:** "The soy sauce and wasabi is good. But try it plain first."

*He watches you try it.*

**JAY:** "Good?"

**YOU:** "Really good."

*He nods, satisfied in a quiet way.*

---

## CHOICE 2 — Eating together. Something gets said.

---

**OPTION A:** *"What made you want to bring someone today?"*

> **YOU:** "Why today? And why me?"
>
> *He sets down his chopsticks.*
>
> **JAY:** "I've been thinking about that line between being alone and being lonely."
>
> **YOU:** "Which side were you on?"
>
> **JAY:** "Neither, exactly. But I thought — if I bring someone, who would it not feel like work?"
>
> *A pause.*
>
> **YOU:** "And I made that list."
>
> **JAY:** "You were the list."
>
> *He picks up his chopsticks again.*
>
> **JAY:** "Being with you doesn't cost anything. I mean that as a compliment."
>
> **YOU:** "I know."
>
> *He looks up. Something in his expression — surprised, a little, that you understood.*

---

**OPTION B:** *"Is there something on your mind?"*

> **YOU:** "You said you were thinking something through."
>
> **JAY:** "Mm."
>
> **YOU:** "Did you figure it out?"
>
> *He looks at the water through the window.*
>
> **JAY:** "I think I've been holding something too tightly."
>
> **YOU:** "What?"
>
> **JAY:** "The version of myself I perform. Even offstage."
>
> *A pause.*
>
> **JAY:** "I don't know when it started. I'm trying to notice when I do it."
>
> **YOU:** "Are you doing it now?"
>
> *He thinks about it. Actually thinks.*
>
> **JAY:** "No."
>
> *He says it like a small discovery.*
>
> **JAY:** "No, I don't think I am."

---

## SCENE 4 — AFTER

*You eat slowly. He talks more than usual — about the album that was playing in the car, about something he read last week, about a sound he heard during rehearsal that he can't get out of his head.*

*Outside, the light has changed. Afternoon now.*

*He goes back to the railing. You follow.*

**NARRATION:**
> *He mentioned a line he wrote here.*
>
> *You're thinking about asking again.*

---

## CHOICE 3 — Before the drive back.

---

**OPTION A:** *"I'm glad you texted me."*

> **YOU:** "I'm glad you included me today."
>
> *He turns from the water.*
>
> **JAY:** "..."
>
> *He looks at you for a moment. Like he's deciding something.*
>
> **JAY:** "The line I wrote here."
>
> **YOU:** "Yeah?"
>
> **JAY:** "'Some things are easier to carry when someone knows you're carrying them.'"
>
> *A pause.*
>
> **JAY:** "I think I wrote it about someone I hadn't met yet."
>
> *He turns back to the water.*

---

**OPTION B:** *"We should do this again."*

> **YOU:** "We should do this again."
>
> *He's quiet for a moment.*
>
> **JAY:** "You'd come again?"
>
> **YOU:** "Obviously."
>
> **JAY:** "..."
>
> *He almost smiles.*
>
> **JAY:** "I'll think of somewhere new."
>
> **YOU:** "I trust your judgment."
>
> **JAY:** "That's either very wise or very unwise."
>
> *He says it softly. Almost to himself.*

---

## DRIVE BACK

*The return is quiet. He puts on a different album — slower. He doesn't explain the choice.*

*Halfway back, he says:*

**JAY:** "You're easy to be around."

**YOU:** "Is that rare for you?"

**JAY:** "..."

**JAY:** "Yes."

**NARRATION:**
> *At some point on the highway, without thinking about it, you fall asleep.*
>
> *When you wake up, you're already back. He's parked. The engine is off.*
>
> *He's writing something in the book from the back seat. He stops when you stir.*

**JAY:** "We're back."

**YOU:** "What were you writing?"

*He closes the book.*

**JAY:** "I'll show you. Eventually."

*Neither of you mentions the day again. But it happened. And you both know that.*
