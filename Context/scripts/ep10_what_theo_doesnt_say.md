# CALIX Episode 10 — "What Theo Doesn't Say"

> **Act:** 2 — Friction
> **Featured:** Theo
> **Setting:** Practice room, after everyone else leaves. Evening light through the high windows going orange.
> **Reward:** Chips bag (from Theo)
> **Stat Gate:** THEO_TRUST ≥ 4

---

## SCENE DIRECTION NOTES

*Tone: Something underneath the warmth. The cost of always being the energy of the room.*
*Music: The practice track, looping, then cutting out. Then nothing. The silence of a room where someone is pushing past what they should.*
*Camera: The mirror. You see Theo's face reflected — different from his face in the room. More.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*Six in the evening. Practice was supposed to end at five.*

*Theo doesn't leave.*

*The others filter out — Kain with his bag and his tablet already open to the next thing, Jay with headphones in before he reaches the door, Finn pausing in the doorway and looking back at Theo in the mirror before he goes. Something in Finn's expression says he's noticed and has decided to let Theo have it.*

*You notice too.*

*You pack up slowly.*

*Theo is running the third section. The floor work — his suggestion, his victory, now his to carry. And he's carrying it correctly. The sequence is right.*

*But there's something about the way he lands on count eight.*

*You've been watching enough practices now to know that something is off.*

---

## SCENE 1 — THE LIMP

*Theo resets.*

*Runs it again.*

*Count eight.*

*The landing — his right foot hits the floor a half-step slow and his weight redistribution does something to compensate. It's subtle. The kind of thing you only catch if you're looking for it, or if you've watched him long enough to know his baseline.*

*His knee.*

*He doesn't grab it. He doesn't stop. He resets and runs it again, and the compensation is there again, and he resets and the track loops and you're standing at the edge of the room watching this.*

**NARRATION:**
> *Theo's relationship to his body looks uncomplicated from the outside — it does what he tells it to.*
>
> *Bodies have limits, though. And Theo, you're learning, does not tell his body about limits.*

## SCENE 2 — THE FLOOR

*You're both on the floor.*

*Theo has the ice pack from the first aid kit on the shelf — he knew exactly where it was, which tells you this isn't the first time — and it's on his knee, and he's looking at the mirror from down here the way you look at a room when you've taken the pressure off.*

*The practice room light is going orange through the high windows.*

*You're close enough that you can hear him breathing past the burn.*

**THEO:**
"Can I tell you something without you making it a thing."

**YOU:**
"Depends on the thing."

**THEO:**
"...fair."

*He shifts the ice pack.*

**THEO:**
"I've been dancing since I was eight. That's fourteen years. And there's not a single year in those fourteen where something wasn't — like, some cost. A thing you push through, or work around, or ignore until it goes away on its own."

*He looks at his knee.*

**THEO:**
"I don't regret it. That's not what I'm saying. I want you to understand that's not what I'm saying."

**YOU:**
"Then what are you saying?"

**THEO:**
"I'm saying — sometimes I wish it didn't cost anything. I wish I could just do this and love it and not have it hurt me at the same time. I wish those two things could both be true at once."

*He says it very quietly.*

**THEO:**
"That's all. It's not a big thing, I know."

---

## SCENE 3 — WHAT THEO ACTUALLY DOESN'T SAY

**YOU:**
"It's a big thing."

*Theo looks at you.*

**YOU:**
"Fourteen years and you're still here at six pm with an ice pack because you don't want to lose the floor sequence you suggested, because you want the work to be as good as you can make it. That's not a small thing, Theo."

*A long pause.*

*Theo looks at the ceiling.*

**THEO:**
"..."

*Something in his face.*

*Theo cries with his face completely still. His eyes are bright in a way that has nothing to do with the practice room light.*

**THEO:**
"Okay."

*He exhales.*

**THEO:**
"Yeah. Thanks for saying that."

## SCENE 4 — CHIPS

*They sit there until the ice pack warms up.*

*Then Theo stands — carefully, testing the knee, satisfied when it holds — and stretches.*

*He looks at you.*

**THEO:**
"Okay. I'm going to go ice this properly and eat something real and go to bed before ten, which hasn't happened in about three months."

*He picks up his bag.*

*He goes to the door. Stops.*

**THEO:**
"You didn't have to stay."

**YOU:**
"I know."

*He looks at you for a moment. Something settled.*

**THEO:**
"Good."

*He leaves.*

*There's no note.*

*You don't need one.*
