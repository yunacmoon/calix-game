# CALIX Episode 10 — "What Theo Doesn't Say"

> **Act:** 2 — Friction
> **Featured:** Theo
> **Setting:** Practice room, after everyone else leaves. Evening light through the high windows going orange.
> **Reward:** Chips bag (from Theo)
> **Stat Gate:** THEO_TRUST ≥ 4

---

## SCENE DIRECTION NOTES

*Tone: Something underneath the warmth. The cost of always being the energy of the room.*
*Music: The practice track, looping, then cutting out. Then nothing. The silence of a room where someone is pushing past what they should.*
*Camera: The mirror. You see Theo's face reflected — different from his face in the room. More.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*Six in the evening. Practice was supposed to end at five.*

*Theo doesn't leave.*

*The others filter out — Kain with his bag and his tablet already open to the next thing, Jay with headphones in before he reaches the door, Finn pausing in the doorway and looking back at Theo in the mirror before he goes, something in his expression that says he's noticed and has decided to let Theo have it.*

*You notice too.*

*You pack up slowly.*

*Theo is running the third section. The one that was added this week. The floor work — his suggestion, his victory, now his to carry. And he's carrying it fine. He's carrying it correctly.*

*But there's something about the way he lands on count eight.*

*You've seen enough practices now to know that something is off.*

---

## SCENE 1 — THE LIMP

*Theo resets.*

*Runs it again.*

*Count eight.*

*The landing — his right foot hits the floor a half-step slow and his weight redistribution is doing something to compensate. It's subtle. It's the kind of thing you only see if you're looking for it, or if you've been watching him for long enough.*

*His knee.*

*He doesn't grab it. He doesn't stop. He resets and does it again, and the compensation is there again, and he resets again and the track is still going and you're standing at the edge of the room watching this.*

**NARRATION:**
> *Theo's relationship to his body is uncomplicated from the outside — it does what he tells it to. Theo dances because the movement means something to him, and his body follows that.*
>
> *But bodies have limits. And Theo, you're learning, does not tell his body about limits.*

---

## SCENE 2 — THE FLOOR

*If you stayed — you're both on the floor now.*

*Theo has the ice pack from the first aid kit on the shelf — he knew exactly where it was, which tells you this isn't the first time — and he's got it on his knee, just holding it there, looking at the mirror from the floor.*

**THEO:**
"Can I tell you something without you making it a thing."

**YOU:**
"Depends on the thing."

**THEO:**
"...fair."

*He shifts the ice pack.*

**THEO:**
"I've been dancing since I was eight. That's fourteen years. And there's not a year where something hasn't been — like, some kind of cost. A thing you push through, or work around, or ignore until it goes away."

*He looks at his knee.*

**THEO:**
"I don't regret it. That's not what I'm saying."

**YOU:**
"What are you saying?"

**THEO:**
"I'm saying — sometimes I wish it didn't cost anything. I wish I could just do the thing I love and have the thing I love not hurt me."

*He says it very quietly. It's the quietest thing Theo has said in your presence.*

**THEO:**
"That's all. It's not a big thing."

---

## SCENE 3 — WHAT THEO ACTUALLY DOESN'T SAY

*A pause.*

**YOU:**
"It's a big thing."

*Theo looks at you.*

**YOU:**
"Fourteen years and you're still here at six pm with an ice pack so you don't lose a floor sequence you suggested because you want the work to be as good as you can make it — that's a big thing, Theo."

*A long pause.*

*Theo looks at the ceiling.*

**THEO:**
"..."

*Something in his face.*

*Theo, you've noticed, cries with his eyes open and his face completely still, the way someone does when they've decided crying is fine but performing crying isn't. His eyes are bright now in a way that has nothing to do with the practice room lights.*

**THEO:**
"Okay. Yeah."

*He exhales.*

**THEO:**
"Thanks for saying that."

## SCENE 4 — CHIPS

*They sit there until the ice pack warms up.*

*Then Theo stands — carefully, testing the knee — and decides he's okay. He stretches.*

*He looks at you.*

**THEO:**
"Okay. I'm going to go ice this properly and eat something and go to bed before ten, which hasn't happened in three months."

*He picks up his bag.*

*He goes to the door. Stops.*

**THEO:**
"You didn't have to stay."

**YOU:**
"I know."

*He looks at you for a moment.*

*He nods. Something settled.*

**THEO:**
"Good."

*He leaves.*
