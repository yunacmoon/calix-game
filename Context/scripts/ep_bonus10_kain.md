# CALIX Special Episode — "The Long Drive" (Kain)

> **Act:** Bonus
> **Featured:** Kain
> **Setting:** A free day. Kain's car. The road to Incheon. A seaside restaurant. The drive back.
> **Reward:** `200 coins`
> **Stat Gate:** none

---

## SCENE DIRECTION NOTES

*Tone: Kain outside his element — driving, not performing, not leading. Just moving through space with you.*
*Music: Whatever's on the radio. He doesn't comment on it. Neither do you. That feels right.*
*Camera: The passenger window. The city giving way to highway giving way to sky.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*You don't expect the text.*

*8:14 AM. A day off — the first real one in three weeks. You were still half asleep.*

*The message is short. No preamble.*

> **KAIN:** Free today?

*You stare at it for a moment.*

*The next one comes before you answer.*

> **KAIN:** I'm driving somewhere. You can come.

*Not an invitation, exactly. Not a question. Just — the door, left open.*

*You get dressed.*

---

## SCENE 1 — THE CAR

*His car is cleaner than you expected. Nothing in the back seat. An air freshener that smells like cedar. He doesn't say anything when you get in, just checks his mirrors and pulls out.*

*The radio is on low — something instrumental. He doesn't touch it.*

*You're thirty minutes out of the city before he says anything.*

**KAIN:** "Never been to the west coast?"

*It's less a question than a checkpoint. Like he already knows the answer and is filing it.*

**NARRATION:**
> *The highway opens up here. Seoul falls behind in the rearview — towers first, then density, then finally sky.*
>
> *Kain drives the way he does everything. Like he's already decided where he's going and the only thing left is getting there.*

---

## CHOICE 1 — An hour in the car. What do you talk about?

---

**OPTION A:** *"Do you do this often? Just — drive?"*

> **YOU:** "Do you do this often? Just drive somewhere?"
>
> *A pause. He considers it.*
>
> **KAIN:** "When things get loud."
>
> **YOU:** "In your head?"
>
> **KAIN:** "Everywhere."
>
> *He changes lanes. Smooth. Unhurried.*
>
> **KAIN:** "Driving is the only thing where I'm not supposed to be doing something else."
>
> *You let that sit. Outside, the city has turned into flat land, pale sky, the occasional crane over a construction site.*
>
> **YOU:** "That makes sense."
>
> **KAIN:** "..."
>
> *He glances over. Just for a second.*
>
> **KAIN:** "Most people ask why I don't just go somewhere closer."
>
> **YOU:** "The distance is part of it."
>
> *He doesn't answer. But something in his posture shifts — barely, almost nothing — like a small weight was put down.*

---

**OPTION B:** *"You didn't say where we're going."*

> **YOU:** "You didn't actually say where we're going."
>
> **KAIN:** "The sea."
>
> **YOU:** "That's it?"
>
> **KAIN:** "What else do you need."
>
> *Not dismissive. Genuine. He means it.*
>
> **YOU:** "I don't know. An ETA. A plan."
>
> **KAIN:** "Two hours. No plan."
>
> *A beat.*
>
> **KAIN:** "You can go back if you want a plan."
>
> **YOU:** "I didn't say I wanted one."
>
> *He almost smiles. You catch the edge of it before it disappears.*
>
> **KAIN:** "Good."

---

## SCENE 2 — THE SEA

*Incheon. The industrial port gives way to a quieter stretch — smaller fishing boats, the smell of salt and something caught and cleaned. He parks in a small lot above a row of restaurants with tanks out front.*

*You walk down to the water first, before eating. He stands at the railing and looks out. Doesn't take out his phone. Just looks.*

*You stand beside him.*

**NARRATION:**
> *The sea here isn't dramatic. It's grey-green and flat under the overcast sky. Seagulls. The sound of boats. A man sorting something on a dock below.*
>
> *Kain has been to a hundred more impressive places. You know this. He knows you know this.*
>
> *He's here anyway.*

**KAIN:** "The light's better on clear days."

**YOU:** "It's not bad today."

**KAIN:** "No."

*A long pause.*

**KAIN:** "Come on. I'm hungry."

---

## SCENE 3 — THE RESTAURANT

*The restaurant is small. Eight tables. A tank of fish near the entrance. The owner recognizes something about Kain — the posture, maybe, or the face — and starts to say something, then decides against it. Kain nods once. They're seated in the back.*

*The raw fish comes out on a wide platter — white flesh, bright against the ice, sesame leaves, fermented soybean paste, the sharp smell of the sea.*

*Kain eats methodically. He wraps a piece in a leaf, precise.*

**KAIN:** "Eat it with the paste first. Then try it plain."

**YOU:** "Are you always this specific about food?"

**KAIN:** "About everything."

*He says it like a fact, not a flaw.*

---

## CHOICE 2 — Eating together. Something gets said.

---

**OPTION A:** *"What made you want to bring someone today?"*

> **YOU:** "Why'd you want company today?"
>
> *He picks up another piece. Sets it down.*
>
> **KAIN:** "I didn't, at first."
>
> **YOU:** "Then?"
>
> **KAIN:** "I thought about who would be quiet enough."
>
> *A pause.*
>
> **YOU:** "And that was me."
>
> **KAIN:** "You don't fill silence to fill it."
>
> *He says it like an observation. Like he's describing a weather pattern.*
>
> **KAIN:** "Most people do. It makes me tired."
>
> **YOU:** "I can talk more, if you want."
>
> *He gives you a look. The flattest look.*
>
> **KAIN:** "Don't."
>
> *You laugh. He doesn't. But there's something at the corner of his mouth.*

---

**OPTION B:** *"Is there something on your mind?"*

> **YOU:** "You seem like you needed to get out."
>
> **KAIN:** "I always need to get out."
>
> **YOU:** "More than usual."
>
> *He's quiet for a moment. Drinks his water.*
>
> **KAIN:** "There's a decision coming. About the group."
>
> **YOU:** "What kind?"
>
> **KAIN:** "The kind where someone has to say something no one wants to hear."
>
> *A beat.*
>
> **YOU:** "And it's going to be you."
>
> **KAIN:** "It's always me."
>
> *He says it without bitterness. Just — accuracy.*
>
> **KAIN:** "I needed a day where I wasn't that."
>
> *He picks up his chopsticks again.*
>
> **KAIN:** "So. We're not talking about it."
>
> **YOU:** "Okay."
>
> **KAIN:** "..."
>
> **KAIN:** "Thank you."
>
> *He says it quietly. Like he's not sure he meant to.*

---

## SCENE 4 — AFTER

*You eat for a long time. The owner brings out extra side dishes without being asked. Kain finishes everything. You talk about small things — a song you heard in the car, whether the fish tastes different closer to the water, a memory he mentions briefly and then doesn't expand on.*

*You don't push.*

*Outside, after, the light has changed. Afternoon now. Pale gold where it wasn't before.*

*He stands at the railing again. This time you don't stand beside him right away — you watch him from a few steps back.*

**NARRATION:**
> *There's a version of Kain that exists only when no one is performing for him.*
>
> *You're starting to know what that version looks like.*

*He turns.*

**KAIN:** "Ready?"

**YOU:** "Yeah."

*He starts toward the car. Then stops.*

---

## CHOICE 3 — Before the drive back.

---

**OPTION A:** *"I'm glad you texted me."*

> **YOU:** "I'm glad you texted me this morning."
>
> *He looks at you.*
>
> **KAIN:** "..."
>
> *A long pause. The wind off the water. A seagull somewhere.*
>
> **KAIN:** "Yeah."
>
> *He turns and keeps walking.*
>
> *But slower, now. Like he's not in a hurry to get back.*

---

**OPTION B:** *"We should do this again."*

> **YOU:** "We should do this again sometime."
>
> **KAIN:** "..."
>
> *He looks out at the water one more time.*
>
> **KAIN:** "Don't make it a thing."
>
> **YOU:** "I'm not."
>
> **KAIN:** "Good."
>
> *A beat.*
>
> **KAIN:** "We'll see."
>
> *Coming from him — that's almost a yes.*

---

## DRIVE BACK

*The return is quieter than the drive out. He puts on something different — not the radio this time but something from his phone. A playlist you don't recognize. Slower than you'd expect.*

*You don't ask about it.*

*He drives.*

*You watch the city come back, piece by piece. The cranes, then the density, then the towers. Seoul reassembling itself around you.*

**NARRATION:**
> *At some point on the highway, without thinking about it, you fall asleep.*
>
> *When you wake up, you're already back. He's parked. The engine is off.*
>
> *He's looking at his phone. He glances up.*

**KAIN:** "You snore."

**YOU:** "I don't."

**KAIN:** "..."

*He gets out of the car.*

*You follow.*

*Neither of you mentions the day again. But it happened. And you both know that.*
